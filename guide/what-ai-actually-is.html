What AI actually is (and isn't)

5 min readPlain English

By the end

You'll know what today's AI can be trusted to do — and exactly where it'll burn you if you're not careful.

Let's clear up what these AI tools — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — actually are, because most bad experiences come from expecting the wrong thing.

Think of AI as an incredibly fast, well-read intern. It has read a huge slice of the internet, so it can write, explain, and summarize almost anything in seconds. But it wasn't in the room yesterday, it doesn't know your business, and — this is the important part — when it doesn't know something, it usually doesn't say so. It just gives you a confident answer that sounds right.

So it's brilliant and a little bit dangerous for the same reason: it always sounds sure of itself.

What it's genuinely great at

  • Turning rough notes into clean writing — emails, posts, summaries
  • Explaining something complicated in plain words
  • Brainstorming ideas and options
  • Tidying and reformatting text
  • Getting you a solid first draft, fast

Where it'll trip you up

  • Exact facts, names, numbers, dates — it can invent them (people call this "hallucinating")
  • Recent events — most tools only know up to a cut-off date unless connected to the web
  • Math and counting — it guesses unless it has a calculator
  • Anything where a confident wrong answer is expensive — legal, medical, money, promises to customers

A quick before / after

Risky: "Is this contract clause legal?" — it gives a confident yes or no, and you can't tell if it's wrong.

Smart: "Explain in plain English what this clause means, and what I should ask a lawyer." Now it's doing what it's good at — explaining — and you keep the judgment.

The rule: let AI do the reading and drafting. You keep the deciding.

The three mistakes almost everyone makes

  • Treating it like Google. It isn't looking things up — it's predicting good-sounding text. Facts still need checking.
  • Assuming it knows your business. It doesn't, until you tell it. (That's Lesson 4.)
  • Believing it because it sounds sure. Confidence is not the same as being right.

Takeaway

AI is a fast, confident intern that read everything, remembers nothing about you, and never admits doubt. Use it for drafting, explaining, and ideas — and double-check anything that has to be exactly right.

Try this today: ask an AI tool to explain one thing from your own work in plain English. Notice what it gets right — and where it's clearly just guessing.
