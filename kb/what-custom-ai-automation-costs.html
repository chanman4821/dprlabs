Building & Owning AI

What does custom AI automation actually cost?

ExplainerLast reviewed Jul 8, 20266 min read

In short

Custom AI automation is normally priced as a one-time setup fee to build and connect it, plus a monthly retainer to run, watch, and improve it. The setup buys the working system; the retainer keeps it working and owned by you. Exact numbers depend on scope — treat any general range as an illustration, not a quote.

"How much does AI automation cost?" is the right question and a hard one to answer honestly, because the price follows the work — and the work depends on your systems, your data, and how much has to be handed over. Here's how the pricing actually breaks down, so you can judge a quote instead of guessing at one.

Why it's a setup fee plus a monthly retainer

A real automation isn't a one-off deliverable you buy once and forget. It has to be built and wired into your tools (that's the setup), and then it has to be watched and adjusted as your data shifts and the underlying AI models change (that's the retainer). Anyone selling a "one payment, done forever" AI system is either overcharging up front for the maintenance, or planning not to do it.

What the setup fee buys

  • Scoping the one workflow and the number it should move
  • Building the system and connecting it to your tools
  • Testing it on real, messy inputs
  • Handover: the code, the accounts, and the docs — so you own it

What the monthly retainer covers

  • Monitoring that it's still working as inputs change
  • Changes and improvements as you learn what's useful
  • Re-measuring the number so the value is proven, not assumed
  • Keeping it current as AI models are updated

How to estimate your own scope

You can size a build yourself, roughly, by asking what makes it bigger or smaller. More integrations, messier data that needs cleaning, and stricter compliance all push the setup up. A single, clean, well-defined workflow keeps it down. Any specific dollar range you read online is an illustration — the honest number comes from scoping your actual workflow.

For reference, DPR publishes fixed starting packages rather than vague "it depends" pricing: a Starter build at $3.5k setup + $1.5k/month, and a Growth build at $15k setup + $6k/month. Those are DPR's own posted terms, not a claim about what your project will cost — your scope sets that.

When automation is worth it — and when it isn't

Automation pays off when a task is repetitive, high-volume, and measurable — the kind of work where shaving minutes per case adds up fast. It's not worth it when the task is rare, the rules are fuzzy, or nobody on your side will own the result. A good partner will tell you when the honest answer is "don't automate this yet."
