# Deeper — Value-Based Landing Page Plan

*Goal: a marketing landing page that clearly says **what we do**, **how we do it**, and
**why it pays off** — grounded in what real AI buyers want in 2025–2026.*

---

## Part 1 — Verdict on the current site: does it make sense?

**Short answer: the shape is right, the proof is fake. Fix the proof and you have a strong page.**

### What already works (keep this)
- **Value-led, not tech-led at the top.** "AI consulting that ships, measured by a number"
  is exactly the promise buyers want — outcomes over demos.
- **Clear story arc:** what we do → how we work → results → who we are → contact.
- **Strong buyer-aligned messaging already present:** ~3-week pilot, "you own it,"
  "no lock-in," "measure honestly." These map directly to what buyers say they want
  (see Part 2). That's real alignment, not luck.
- **Good craft:** accessibility (skip link, reduced-motion, ARIA), reduced-motion fallbacks,
  semantic structure. This is above average.

### What breaks it (the #1 problem)
- **Everything used as proof is invented:**
  - Fake client logos (Northwind, Meridian, Vantage, Atlas, Cobalt, Verge, Helio, Kestrel).
  - Fake testimonials with real-sounding names (Maya Ellison, "Head of Ops, Northwind").
  - Fake founders (Elena Marsh, Theo Vasquez, Sana Okafor) with fake bios.
  - Round, unverifiable stats (40+ shipped, 92% kept, $240k removed, 11,000 hours).
- **Why this matters:** the page's core promise is *"proof by a number."* If a buyer
  emails "Northwind" or googles a founder and finds nothing, the whole promise collapses.
  Fabricated proof on a trust-based service is the single biggest risk here.

### What's misaligned (secondary fixes)
- **Feature/tech-forward in the middle** ("Agent Orchestrator," "AI POS," "AI SDR").
  Buyers don't buy agents — they buy *fewer hours on invoices* and *faster support replies*.
  Lead with the outcome, mention the tech second.
- **No risk reversal / no pricing signal.** Buyers fear AI projects that overpromise.
  A clear "here's the offer, here's what it costs, here's the guarantee" beats more adjectives.
- **No real case study.** Even one true, specific story outperforms eight fake logos.
- **The "Deeper / surface vs deep" metaphor is elegant but can go vague.** Keep it as
  flavor, but every section must still answer "what do I get and what does it cost me."

## Part 2 — What buyers actually want (research-grounded)

**What SMBs/mid-market want from AI in 2025–2026:**
- Practical, affordable, **measurable ROI** — not novelty. Quick pilot wins first, then scale.
- **Automate the repetitive work:** support replies, invoices, data entry, document handling.
- **Marketing/content, sales, and turning their own data into insight.**
- **No vendor lock-in** and **knowledge transfer** — they want to own it, not rent it forever.
- **Data security / trust** — especially with their own data.

**How buyers judge an AI consulting group (and how to win):**
- Relevant track record + references + *real* proof of business impact.
- Clear objectives tied to KPIs, not "tech for tech's sake."
- Start small, prove value, scale fast (pilot-first).
- Change management + upskilling the internal team.
- Plan for handover; avoid black-box/lock-in.

**The big failure modes buyers fear (sell against these):** unclear objectives, bad data,
pilots that never reach production, overpromising, and staying dependent on the vendor.

> Takeaway: the page should sound like the antidote to a failed AI project —
> small scope, real number, fast, you keep it.

## Part 3 — The core value proposition (the spine of the page)

**What we do (one sentence):**
> We build AI that does real work in your business — and we prove it in weeks, measured
> in hours saved or dollars freed.

**How we do it (one sentence):**
> One small senior team. We start with a short paid pilot on your biggest time-sink,
> measure it honestly, and hand it to your team to own. No lock-in.

**Five value pillars (each maps to a buyer need above):**
1. **Proof in weeks, not slideware.** A working pilot in ~3 weeks, scored by a real number.
2. **We take the work your team dreads.** Support replies, invoices, documents, data entry.
3. **You own it in the end.** Training, playbooks, runs in your stack, no lock-in.
4. **Senior people only.** You work with the builders, not an account manager.
5. **Safe and grounded.** Your data, guardrails, a human on the calls that matter.

## Part 4 — Page structure, section by section

For each: **goal · headline direction · what it says · proof · call-to-action.**

1. **Hero**
   - Goal: state the promise + get the click.
   - Headline: *"AI that does real work — proven in weeks, measured in a number."*
   - Sub: the "how we do it" sentence from Part 3.
   - Proof: **one honest stat or one real pilot result** (not eight fake logos).
   - CTA: primary "Book a free opportunity read" · secondary "See how we work ↓".

2. **The problem (empathy hook)**
   - Goal: show you get why their last AI effort stalled.
   - Headline: *"Most AI projects die as a demo."*
   - Says: vendors sell slideware; pilots never reach production; teams stay dependent.
   - CTA: none — just earn the nod.

3. **What we do (outcome-framed services)**
   - Goal: 3–5 services, each named by the *outcome*, tech mentioned second.
   - Examples: "Take the busywork off your team (automation)," "Turn your data into
     forecasts you can act on," "Custom AI wired into your real systems," "Your team
     owns it (training + handover)."
   - Proof: a number per service — but only **real** numbers.
   - CTA: per-row "Talk about this →".

4. **How we work (the path)**
   - Goal: make the engagement feel safe and concrete.
   - Steps: Dive in (audit) → Build a pilot (~3 wks) → Measure honestly → Scale & hand off.
   - Honesty hook: *"If the numbers aren't there, we say so and change course."*
   - CTA: "Start with a pilot →".

5. **Proof (real, see Part 5)**
   - Goal: the trust section. One or two **true** case studies with a number + context,
     or honest early-stage framing. No invented clients.

6. **Why us**
   - Goal: senior team, no lock-in, knowledge transfer, data safety.
   - Real founder bios (or a real "who you'll work with"), stated plainly.

7. **The offer + risk reversal**
   - Goal: kill the "what if it fails" fear.
   - Says: the free opportunity read, then a fixed-scope paid pilot, with a guarantee
     ("if the pilot misses the number we agreed, you don't pay for it").
   - This is the strongest single addition vs. the current site.

8. **FAQ (objection handling)**
   - Cost range, data security, lock-in, time commitment, what happens after the pilot.

9. **Final CTA + contact form**
   - Goal: low-friction next step. "Tell us one task your team dreads."
   - Keep the 1-business-day reply + free read promise.

## Part 5 — Proof strategy (the fix for the trust problem)

**Rule: never invent a client, quote, logo, founder, or stat.** Use what is true today:

- **If you have real clients:** 1–2 named or anonymized case studies — situation, what you
  built, the measured number, a real quote with permission. One true story beats eight logos.
- **If you're early / pre-revenue:** say so with confidence instead of faking it:
  - Replace fake logos with *"What a first pilot looks like"* — a concrete worked example.
  - Replace fake testimonials with a **live demo** or a short loom of a real build.
  - Use a **guarantee** as proof of confidence (risk reversal in section 7).
  - Real founder bios — your actual background is more persuasive than invented ones.
- **Stats:** only show numbers you can defend. "~3 weeks to a pilot" (your process) is fine.
  "$240k removed" needs a real engagement behind it or it goes.

## Part 6 — Messaging do / don't + CTA ladder

**Do:** lead with outcomes · use plain words · one number per claim · name the buyer's pain ·
show the offer and the guarantee · keep "you own it / no lock-in" loud.

**Don't:** open with tech product names · fabricate proof · stack adjectives ·
hide the price/offer · promise "transformation" with no number.

**CTA ladder (low → high commitment):**
1. *Free opportunity read* — "Tell us one task your team dreads." (top of page + closing)
2. *Paid pilot* — fixed scope, ~3 weeks, one number to hit.
3. *Book a call* — calendar link in the nav, always reachable.

3. Add the offer + guarantee section (biggest trust win).

5. Add FAQ. 6. Keep the existing craft (motion, a11y, layout) — it's good.
