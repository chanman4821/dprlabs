## 1. What DPR is (the dual model + the two flywheels)

DPR AI ("Deeper," dprai.io) runs two connected lines of business. The ethos across both is the same: **owned, measured, honest — proof over theater.**

**Line 1 — Build & consult on owned, measured AI.** We wire real AI inside a company's actual systems, judge it on one honest number the client agrees to up front, and hand it over so the client owns it. No lock-in. We sell this as retainers, not one-off projects, across four concrete service pillars:

| Pillar | What we do | The honest number |
|---|---|---|
| AI automation | Automate a real workflow (support, ops, back-office) end to end | Hours saved / tickets deflected |
| AI compliance & governance | Regulatory, risk, audit-ready AI controls and monitoring | Findings caught / audit pass rate |
| Low-code / no-code builds | Ship working internal tools and apps fast on proven platforms | Time-to-live, adoption |
| Data & systems integration | Connect the systems so the AI has clean, wired-in data | Data coverage / sync reliability |

**Line 2 — Connect founders with capital.** We bring startup founders and investors together and build elite, high-trust relationships on both sides. We do **introductions and information only** — we never negotiate terms, recommend securities, advise investors, or touch anyone's money. The capital side earns money exactly one way: a **flat, recurring dealflow subscription** paid by investors (SaaS, not a cut of any raise). We never take a percentage of a raise, a per-intro success fee, or any compensation tied to a financing event.

**Where equity fits — and where it never does.** We sometimes take **equity/SAFE, but only as payment for build work delivered**, priced to the fair cash value of those services and documented as in-lieu-of-cash. Equity is consideration for Line 1 services, full stop. We never grant or receive equity for making an introduction, for a raise, or for anything on the capital-connection side. That strict separation is what keeps the equity clean and outside broker-dealer territory.

**The firewall between the two lines.** Because we can hold equity in a startup we build for and separately charge investors for curated dealflow, we hard-separate the two so we are never paid to promote our own book. The rule: **deals in which DPR holds equity are a small, capped, clearly-labeled fraction of any subscriber's feed — and we do not charge for access to them.** We publish our curation standard (reject rate; share of externally-sourced vs. portfolio deals) so subscribers know they are buying independent judgment, not our position.

### The two flywheels

**The build flywheel:** good builds create real, measured traction → traction produces honest case studies → case studies attract better clients and better founders.

**The relationship flywheel (the real moat):** each good build makes a founder genuinely fundable → we make a warm, vetted introduction to an investor in our network → investors get de-risked, AI-validated signal → wins and warm intros deepen trust on both sides → better founders and better investors keep pulling in. **The durable asset is the relationship network itself, not any single deal.** Every build and every introduction compounds it. Neither side is easy to copy, because both rest on the same slowly-earned thing: trust.

### Why the combo beats either alone

A pure AI-services shop lives project-to-project with no compounding asset. A pure connector has no proof — just deal spam — and drifts into securities-law trouble the moment it charges for outcomes. Together, the AI builds are the *proof engine* that earns the right to make introductions, and the investor network is the *distribution* that makes those builds more valuable. Retainer MRR funds the studio; the recurring investor subscription is the compliant capital-side upside; equity is booked only as service payment, never as a raise fee.

## 2. Who we serve

We are deliberately narrow. **For the first 12–18 months the build side serves one wedge vertical: regulated, data-sensitive businesses** (legal, healthcare, fintech, insurance). Specialists retain longer and charge more than generalists, and our compliance & governance pillar is strongest exactly here. Two adjacent segments are **explicitly deferred to a later phase — not current ICP**:

| ICP | Stage / type | Phase |
|---|---|---|
| **Regulated SMBs & mid-market** (wedge) | Legal, healthcare, fintech, insurance — compliance-heavy | **Now** — premium for audit-ready AI |
| Post–PMF startups | Seed–Series B, real revenue and ops pain | Later — some become studio bets |
| Ops-heavy traditional | E-commerce, services, logistics back-office | Later — automation + integration |

### Two founder populations we keep separate on purpose

We work with two groups of founders, and they are **not** the same cohort — blurring them is how the numbers stop adding up:

- **Cash-paying founders** — funded startups that pay a normal cash retainer for build + fundraise-ready advisory. They are recurring MRR, and a **smaller cohort** than the overall build client base.
- **Equity/SAFE "studio" founders** — cash-poor, high-potential teams who take discounted builds **in exchange for a SAFE, in lieu of cash fees.** They are **lumpy, expected-value-zero-to-lottery upside, booked entirely outside MRR** and never counted as recurring cash.

The studio founders are where both flywheels touch: a bet that scales can later convert into a cash-paying enterprise client. But we recognize their value only when it is realized — never as monthly revenue.

### Capital side (investors)

| ICP | Type | What they buy |
|---|---|---|
| Angels & angel groups | Individual accredited investors | Curated, AI-scored research + occasional vetted intro, by subscription |
| Micro-VCs & emerging managers | Sub-$50M funds needing a sourcing edge | Filtered pipeline by sector/stage/check size, diligence memos |
| Family offices | Direct-investing, relationship-driven | High-trust, low-noise access; co-invest rights on higher tiers |
| Syndicate leads | Aggregating other angels | Vetted deals + published methodology to lead confidently |

All investors are **verified accredited**, so introductions stay inside private-placement exemptions. We publish our scoring methodology, disclose every conflict — any stake we hold, any founder-staff tie — on the deal page, and, per the firewall above, keep portfolio deals a capped, unpaid share of the feed. The bridge between the two sides is not a marketing slogan; it is the same relationships, earned build by build.

## 3. The offers

Every offer is labeled **RECURRING** (predictable MRR) or **LUMPY** (one-time or upside). The rule that governs everything: recurring retainers and subscriptions *are* the business; lumpy fees are gravy. We never promise a raise, never take a % of capital raised, never touch investor money, and — read this twice — **no DPR fee is ever triggered by, contingent on, or timed to a financing event.** To stay narrow, we **launch with three packages** (Starter, Growth, and the Signal investor tier); everything below is the full menu we grow into after the first hire, not the day-one SKU list.

### (a) AI build & consulting ladder

Every engagement leads with a **setup fee (LUMPY)** to stand the system up, then a **monthly retainer (RECURRING)** covering monitoring, model/prompt retraining, eval upkeep, and a defined number of deliverables. You recur from day one, not after the project. The prices below are the **single source of truth** — every contribution, $/hr, and headcount figure in Sections 4–5 is derived from exactly these numbers.

| Tier | Pillar(s) | Who it's for | What's delivered | Price |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Starter** | Automation, low-code | Solo founders, small teams | 1 productized build (chatbot, RAG pipeline, or monthly AI insights report), monitoring, monthly check-in | **$3.5k setup + $1.5k/mo** |
| **Growth** | Automation + integration | Funded startups, SMBs | Custom build wired into your systems, retraining, evals, up to 3 deliverables/mo, one honest KPI | **$15k setup + $6k/mo** |
| **Compliance** | Governance & compliance | Regulated (legal, health, fintech) | Growth + audit trail, model-risk docs, human-in-loop controls, quarterly review | **$40k setup + $12k/mo** |
| **Enterprise** | All four | Scaling companies | Multi-system integration, dedicated cadence, SLA, owned handover (no lock-in) | **$50k setup + $20k/mo** |

Setup fees are LUMPY; all monthly tiers are RECURRING. **Compliance and Enterprise are volume-capped, not volume goals** — they are evidence-heavy, bespoke work (35–60 hrs/mo, not 80% template), so a solo/small team carries **at most 2–3 compliance logos** before quality slips. Early MRR is therefore **mostly Starter/Growth** with 1–2 premium anchors; the regulated wedge is our *positioning*, not the near-term revenue base. Deliverables are named and fixed-scope so every invoice is justified.

### (b) Founder side — traction advisory (decoupled from any raise)

We help founders turn a real AI build into real traction and clean instrumentation. This is generic ops/metrics work — **sold and priced without reference to any active raise**, precisely so it can never be characterized as facilitating a financing.

| Offer | Type | What's delivered | Price |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Traction Sprint** | LUMPY | 4-week package: metrics dashboard, one honest number instrumented, data-room hygiene, narrative review | **$8k one-time** |
| **Traction Retainer** | RECURRING | Ongoing build + advisory to keep metrics and story current | **$4k–$6k/mo, billed monthly regardless of any raise** |
| **Studio / equity option** | LUMPY (upside) | Cash-poor, high-potential teams: discounted **build services** for a **SAFE (standard cap)** | Equity strictly in lieu of cash for services rendered |

**On the "optional success component" — the honest version.** There is no success fee DPR can legally take. Any pay conditioned on a round closing is contingent, transaction-based compensation — the SEC tests for the success-contingency and the nexus to a securities transaction, *not* whether it is a flat dollar or a percentage. So the only compliant "upside on a raise" runs two ways: (1) a **registered broker-dealer partner** who contracts with and is paid directly by the founder — never DPR; or (2) **equity/SAFE taken as payment for build services**, priced to fair service value and documented in-lieu-of-cash. We never grant or receive equity for making an introduction.

> **Honest legal caveat (not legal advice):** We are not a broker-dealer and do not guarantee any raise. Taking pay tied to a financing — flat or percentage, ours or a founder's — while unregistered is broker-dealer activity that can expose everyone, including the founder, to SEC/state enforcement and investor rescission rights. Our default is fixed advisory plus warm introductions with **zero** contingent fee.

### (c) Investor side — dealflow & relationship membership

A flat **RECURRING subscription** for verified-accredited investors: curated, AI-validated deals plus access to the elite founder network. **Never** a cut of capital deployed — subscription only, because contingent pay triggers broker-dealer registration.

| Tier | Who | What's included | Price (monthly equiv.) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Signal** | Angels | AI-scored teardowns, published scoring methodology, monthly research memo | **$3k/yr (~$250/mo)** |
| **Syndicate** | Micro-VCs, family offices | Signal + diligence reports, founder update rooms, quarterly pitch day | **$7.5k/yr (~$625/mo)** |
| **Inner Circle** | Active lead investors | Syndicate + co-invest access, personalized sector/stage/check-size filtering, direct intros | **$12k/yr (~$1,000/mo)** |

Subscriptions **bill annually** — so we report them as ARR and model renewal separately from the 4%/mo retainer churn, not as smooth "monthly" cash. Assume a normal SaaS mix skewed to the entry tier (~80% Signal / 15% Syndicate / 5% Inner Circle), which blends to **~$340/mo per subscriber — not $550** — and correspondingly lowers the capital-side contribution at every rung in Section 4. Early on we sell **research and teardowns, not access-to-deals**, until independent (non-portfolio) sourcing volume is real. Every deal page carries the disclaimer stack and discloses every conflict; scores are **information, not recommendations.**

**How the offers compound:** services (a) produce real evidence → founders become fundable on merit (b) → that earned credibility attracts subscribing investors (c) → investor relationships pull in better founders and future enterprise clients. Recurring retainers and subscriptions fund the business; equity (for services) and non-contingent advisory are the compliant upside — booked outside MRR.

## 4. The MRR math

**One price list drives everything below.** AI retainers: **Starter $1.5k/mo, Growth $6k/mo, Compliance $18k/mo, Enterprise $25k/mo** (setup fees are LUMPY and excluded). Investor subs are annual — **Signal $3k/yr ($250/mo), Syndicate $7.5k/yr ($625/mo), Inner Circle $12k/yr ($1,000/mo)** — so they are **ARR-derived**, not true monthly cash.

**What counts as MRR (strict rule).** A dollar is below only if it is **cash, contractually recurring, non-contingent, committed ≥3 months.** Excluded on purpose: **success fees** (contingent raise comp is broker-dealer activity), **equity/SAFE** (lumpy, illiquid), and **cash-poor bridge founders who pay in equity** (studio bets, not subscribers). Three streams qualify:

- **AI retainers** — the engine. Early book is **mostly Starter/Growth** (the wedge is positioning, not the near-term revenue base); Compliance/Enterprise are **capacity-capped anchors** added only from ~$25k on.
- **Investor subscriptions** — realistic SaaS mix skews to the entry tier: **~70% Signal, 20% Syndicate, 10% Inner Circle → blended ~$400/mo per sub** (ARR/12), well under any $550 figure.
- **Cash fundraise-ready founder retainers** — a **separate, smaller** cohort paying **cash $4k–$6k/mo**. Equity founders never appear here.

| Milestone | AI retainers (mix) | Investor subs (~$400/mo) | Cash founder retainers | Blended MRR | Timing (solo start) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| $1k | 1 Starter pilot ($1k) | 0 | 0 | **~$1.0k** | Mo 2–3 |
| $5k | 3 Starter ($4.5k) | 2 ($0.6k) | 0 | **~$5.1k** | Mo 5–8 |
| $10k | 2 Starter + 1 Growth ($9k) | 3 ($1.0k) | 0 | **~$10.0k** | Mo 9–13 |
| $25k | 3 Starter + **1 Compliance** ($22.5k) | 6 ($2.4k) | 0 | **~$24.9k** | Mo 14–20 |
| $50k | 2 Growth + **2 Compliance** ($48k) | 5 ($2.0k) | 0 | **~$50.0k** | Mo 20–30 (first hire) |
| $100k | 5 Growth + **3 Compliance** ($84k) | 20 ($8.4k) | 2 × $5k ($10k) | **~$102k** | Mo 30–42 (small team) |
| $200k | 8 Growth + **4 Compliance + 2 Enterprise** ($170k) | 35 ($15.8k) | 3 × $5k ($15k) | **~$201k** | Mo 42–60 (team of 6–8) |

**The equity-founder refill, shown explicitly.** An earlier draft booked 6–12 "founder retainers" as recurring cash. Stripping equity founders out removes **~$18k–$30k of phantom MRR** at the top rungs. It is refilled **in the counts above**, not by hand-waving: the $100k rung carries **3 Compliance anchors + 20 subs + only 2 cash founders**; the $200k rung adds **2 Enterprise logos + 15 more subs** rather than a dozen equity bets. That is more logos and more sales load — an honestly harder ladder.

**Timing is split by sales cycle.** Regulated buyers run **3–9-month** procurement, so they cannot seed Month 1; Months 1–6 use faster-closing, less-regulated Starter/Growth logos, and compliance revenue lands from ~Month 9.

**Concentration risk — retainers AND subs.** On a tiny base, churn is a cliff. At **$5k, losing one $1.5k Starter is −30%**; at **$25k, losing the single $18k Compliance anchor is −72%** and months of rebuild. Investor subs bill **annually**, so a lost account is a **once-a-year renewal cliff**, not smooth 4%/mo churn — model sub renewal at **~75–80%/yr** separately, and never let prepaid annual cash flatter "monthly recurring."

**Investor-sub acquisition is the hard number.** 35 paid verified-accredited subs is a distribution problem: budget **free-memo → paid conversion of ~2–3%**, so ~**1,200–1,700 readers** feed 35 subscribers, built over years through one repeatable channel (weekly teardown + warm referrals). Assume real CAC in senior time, not zero.

**Lumpy upside (outside MRR, never promised).** SAFE/equity marks are **expected-value zero-to-lottery** — recognize value **only on a realized liquidity event**, never in cash or runway planning. Compliant flat advisory (billed regardless of any raise) is the only other upside — also outside MRR.

## 5. Unit economics & honest ceiling

Recurring revenue is real only if margin survives the labor — and **labor is not flat.** The premium tiers that anchor the margin story are the least template-able.

**Per-tier economics (driven by the Section 4 price list):**

| Tier | Price/mo | Infra pass-through | Delivery hrs/mo | Contribution (pre-your-time) | $/hr (delivery only) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | $1,500 | ~$150 | ~8 | ~$1,350 (90%) | ~$169 |
| Growth | $6,000 | ~$400 | ~15 | ~$5,600 (93%) | ~$373 |
| Compliance | $18,000 | ~$900 | **~40** | ~$17,100 (95%) | ~$428 |
| Enterprise | $25,000 | ~$1,500 | **~60** | ~$23,500 (94%) | ~$392 |

The $/hr looks healthy everywhere, but Compliance/Enterprise **consume 40–60 hrs each** of bespoke model-risk docs and human-in-loop controls — not 12-hour, 80%-template work. The binding constraint is not margin per hour; it is that **a solo carries only ~2–3 compliance logos** before quality (and the moat) collapses.

**Fully-loaded effective rate (the honest one).** A senior solo has ~160 hrs/mo but only **~110–130 billable**; the rest is sales, ops, and curation the retainer must fund. Loaded across active retainers, the true rate falls to **~$150–$210/hr, ~65–75% margin** — strong, not the raw delivery figure.

**Curation is mostly fixed — but not free.** One memo serves every subscriber; budget **~15–20 hrs/mo** to source/vet ~2 deals to memo-ready (~$3–3.5k opportunity cost). At the **entry Signal price of $250/mo**, curation break-even is **~14–16 subs, not 8.** Higher tiers reintroduce a **partial per-subscriber cost**: Inner Circle's personalized, thesis-specific filtering adds a few hours per active lead.

**The honest ceiling before you must hire:**

- **Solo ≈ 6–8 AI retainers (max 2–3 compliance) + ~15 subs ≈ $30k–$40k MRR.** Past that, quality slips.
- **First hire (~$40k MRR).** Regulated-AI talent is expensive: **~$160k–$220k loaded**, not $120k–$160k, and new hires are **~50% billable in months 1–3** (ramp lag). Fund **~$50k–$70k of cash cushion** to hire *ahead* of the ceiling. Capacity roughly doubles to **~$80k–$90k**, dropping blended margin to **~55–60%**.

**Headcount at $200k — run two ways.** *Balanced book (base table):* ~400 delivery hrs + ~20 curation + ~60 sales/ops ≈ 480 hrs ÷ ~110 billable ≈ **~4 delivery staff + a founder on sales = 6.** *Premium-heavy (if you chase compliance logos):* the same $200k on 30–60-hr regulated logos runs **800+ hrs → 8–10 people**, and the loaded margin **falls toward 45–50%.** The 6-person figure holds **only** if compliance logos stay capped — which is exactly why quality, not logo count, protects the line.

**Guardrails baked into the numbers:** price as a retainer from day one; **cap compliance logos per head**; keep subs flat-fee with success fees out of MRR; recognize equity **only on realized exits**; model **annual sub renewal separately** from retainer churn; and **hire ahead** of the ~$40k ceiling so the relationship moat never degrades to chase the next rung.

## 6. Getting both sides from cold (chicken-and-egg)

A two-sided network is worthless empty, and you cannot fake either side. Solve it by **not starting two-sided.** Start as a paid AI-services shop; let real builds *manufacture* the first founders and the proof that earns investor trust.

**Seed the founder side first (the cheaper side).** Your first "founders" are just your first services clients — early-stage companies you build owned, measured AI for. A handful of green builds buys what a cold connector can't: case studies, standing relationships, and a de-risked startup you understand.

**Seed the investor side with information, not deals.** A free weekly "what we're seeing" teardown attracts angels, family offices, and micro-VCs who want signal, not spam. That audience becomes your first **dealflow subscribers** — never success-fee marks.

**The investor side is a distribution problem — the hardest number in the plan.** Reaching **~45 paying, verified-accredited subscribers** against AngelList, syndicates, and free VC content is not guaranteed by good memos. Model it explicitly:

- **Channel:** one owned audience (weekly memo + LinkedIn/X) plus warm 1:1 outreach from build relationships. No paid ads — accredited-investor CAC is punishing.
- **Funnel:** ~1–2% of readers convert, so 45 subs implies a **3,000–4,500-reader list** — a 2–3 year build.
- **CAC:** effectively **20–40 unbillable founder-hours per paying sub**, competing with delivery hours.
- **Renewal churn:** subs bill annually — model **~75–85% annual renewal** separately from 4%/mo retainer churn, and treat these as **ARR-derived**, not smooth MRR.

Honest read: 45 paid accredited subs by month 42–60 works **only** if the memo becomes a genuinely-followed research product, and it likely lands later than the retainer rungs.

**Honest 90-day cold start (two-sided):**

| Weeks | Founder side | Capital side |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | Land 1 paid AI build in your wedge; start the case study | Launch the free weekly memo; build a 100-name investor list |
| 5–8 | Ship, measure the one honest number, get testimonial + referral | Grow to ~50–100 readers; interview 5 investors on what they'd pay for |
| 9–12 | 2nd build in motion; pick your best client as first "studio" candidate | Convert **2–3** readers to a paid sub; hand-share 1 vetted deal *for free* |

Goal by day 90: **one paying build, one repeatable memo, a couple of paying/interested investors, and zero securities exposure** — not a marketplace.

## 7. Delivering it (automated where possible)

**Consulting side — productized, low-touch.** Sell named, fixed-scope packages (automation buildout, RAG/insights pipeline, compliance review, integration wiring), each ~80% template and ~20% per-client config. Every engagement leads with a **retainer** (monitoring, retraining, eval upkeep, set deliverables), so revenue recurs from day one.

**Capital side — AI does the grunt work, you do the judgment.**

| Stage | What AI does | What stays human |
|---|---|---|
| Source | Aggregate startups, dedupe, tag by sector/stage/check-size | Curation taste; conflict checks |
| Vet | Draft diligence memos, score against a published rubric, flag risks | Final scoring; anything reaching an investor |
| Validate | Cross-check founder claims against real traction/build data | The introduction itself |
| Match | Filter each investor's feed to their thesis | The relationship, never automated |

Automation never negotiates terms, recommends a security, or touches money. **Guard the advice line:** every score and memo carries a contractual header — *information, not a recommendation; not investment advice; do your own diligence* — the methodology is published, and counsel reviews whether repeated scoring/validation edges toward investment-adviser territory in your states. The durable asset — **the relationships** — is built by hand and only *supported* by software.

## 8. Honest risks & what we never promise

**Broker-dealer / finder's-fee — the single biggest trap.** There is no adopted US federal finder exemption; **contingent, transaction-based pay tied to capital raised is the SEC's hallmark of unregistered broker activity**, reaffirmed by 2025 settlements. The capital side monetizes **only** a flat recurring dealflow subscription. **No percentage of a raise, no per-intro success fee, ever.**

**The firewall must break the economic nexus, not just the fee label.** DPR can hold equity (as service payment) in a startup and separately charge investors for dealflow — and a raise marks up that SAFE. A "no fee for intros" label does **not** cure that substance-over-form nexus. So the rule is stricter: **we do not warm-introduce a company we hold equity in to any paying subscriber.** If such a match is genuinely investor-driven, it routes entirely through a **registered broker-dealer partner** who contracts and is paid directly — never DPR — with the stake disclosed. Get counsel on whether equity-for-services plus downstream intros is integrated broker activity.

**Decouple advisory from the raise.** "Get-fundable" branding timed to an active round and bundled with investor intros can be characterized as facilitating capital-raising even with a flat fee. So advisory is sold as **generic traction/ops instrumentation**, billed and payable whether or not you raise, with fundraise framing dropped from any offer that co-occurs with intros.

**Conflicts & adverse selection.** Every stake, paid placement, or staff-founder tie is **disclosed on the deal page**; portfolio deals stay a small, capped, unpaid fraction of the feed. Warm intros beat paid finders; over-charging founders for intros signals a weak network.

**What we never promise:** no guaranteed raise, no invented clients/logos/testimonials/stats, no "fully autonomous AI." Every deal carries the disclaimer stack: *not a broker-dealer, not investment advice, no guaranteed raise, all investments are speculative and may lose value, past results don't predict the future.* This is not legal advice — get counsel before launch.

## Still worth pressure-testing

- **2, 3a, 4, 5** (high): The wedge (regulated legal/health/fintech/insurance) is exactly the segment that will NOT buy Compliance/Enterprise from a cold solo. Those buyers gate vendors on SOC 2, cyber insurance, DPAs/BAAs (HIPAA), model-risk references, and 3–9mo security review. A solo/2-person shop cannot pass procurement, yet the margin story and the $25k rung (Mo14–20) lean on a single $18k Compliance anchor. The anchor tiers are the least attainable, not just capacity-capped. → *Reclassify Compliance/Enterprise as ‘after credibility + insurance + first reference logo + likely a regulated-domain partner,’ push their first appearance to ~$50k+ rung, and rebuild the $25k rung on Starter/Growth + subs only. Add SOC2/insurance/BAA readiness as an explicit prerequisite line item and cost it.*
- **1, 8** (high): The stated moat (relationship flywheel) is: build → founder becomes fundable → warm-intro that founder to investors in the network. But those fundable founders are precisely the discounted equity/SAFE ‘studio’ founders DPR holds stock in — and Section 8’s firewall forbids warm-introducing any company DPR holds equity in to a paying subscriber. The firewall therefore guts the core flywheel; the two sections directly contradict each other. → *Pick one honestly: either (a) the moat is externally-sourced dealflow you have NO equity in (then stop selling the ‘we build them then fund them’ story), or (b) equity clients route only through a registered BD partner and are excluded from the paid feed — in which case the flywheel’s compounding value must be re-argued without self-introductions.*
- **1, 3, 7** (high): ‘Owned, no lock-in, we hand it over’ is in tension with the load-bearing claim that $6k–$20k/mo retainers recur indefinitely. Once a productized build is stable and client-owned, the ongoing justification (monitoring/retraining/evals) rarely sustains premium retainer pricing — churn-to-project risk is understated, and the entire MRR thesis rests on this recurrence holding. → *Quantify why retraining/eval upkeep is continuously worth $6k+/mo (model drift cadence, regulatory re-cert, deliverable count), separate a lower ‘steady-state monitoring’ price from the active-build price, and stress-test MRR with a realistic ‘build stabilizes, retainer steps down’ churn scenario.*
