## Section 2 — The one-number principle

- **Eyebrow:** The one-number principle
- **Headline:** If we can't name the number, the pilot isn't ready.
- **Subhead:** Most AI projects fail because nobody agreed what "working" means.
- **Body:** So we don't start until we've written down one measurable outcome — reply time,
  minutes per invoice, hours saved per week — with the exact way we'll measure it. One number
  keeps everyone honest: it decides, in plain sight, whether the pilot worked. That number is
  agreed in writing before any build. If we can't name it, the pilot isn't ready, and we'll
  tell you plainly instead of taking the work.
- **Margin note:** Good numbers are specific and countable. "Feels faster" is not a number.
  "Minutes per invoice, sampled over 400 invoices" is.

## Section 3 — A pilot, week by week

- **Eyebrow:** A pilot, week by week
- **Headline:** Four honest steps. No slide deck at the end.
- **Subhead:** You see real output every few days — not a reveal at the end. Every step has a
  fixed output and no lock-in waiting behind it.

**Step 1 — Agree the number.**
We pick the one workflow and the one metric, set the baseline from your real data, and write
the target and the measurement method down. The fixed price is quoted here — before any
build. If the workflow has no clean number, this is where we say so.
- *When:* Week 0 · *Output:* Scoped number + fixed quote · *Who:* You and a senior builder

**Step 2 — Build on your real systems.**
We build the working system inside your infrastructure, your repo, your accounts — not a
sandbox. It's real, working agentic AI aimed at the number we agreed. You see progress every
few days, so there's no black box.
- *When:* Weeks 1–2 · *Price:* $8k–$15k (fixed, quoted in step 1) · *Output:* A running system

**Step 3 — Measure honestly.**
We run it against the agreed number on a real, human-checked sample, and publish the method
so you can re-check it yourself. We report the same number, the same way, as the baseline. If
it didn't move, we say so — and the guarantee applies.
- *When:* Weeks 2–3 · *Output:* A scorecard (before → after → sample → method → caveat)

**Step 4 — Hand it over — you own it.**
We harden what works, write the playbook, and train your team to run and change it. Code,
weights, and docs are yours — no license to renew, no seat to rent.
- *When:* Weeks 3–4 · *Lock-in:* None · *Output:* Your repo + a team that can run it

## Section 4 — Open measurement & ownership

- **Eyebrow:** Open measurement & ownership
- **Headline:** A number you can't re-check isn't proof.
- **Body:** We set the baseline from your real data before we touch anything, then report the
  same number the same way at the end. We write the method down — sample size, who checked it,
  and the caveats — so you, or anyone, can reproduce it. When we hand off, you own the code
  and the knowledge. You can fire us and keep everything: the system runs on your infra, your
  repo, your accounts, with no dependency on us to keep it alive.
- **Margin note:** "You own it" is architecture, not a promise. We build on your accounts from
  day one so there is nothing to migrate at handover.

---

## Section 5 — When a pilot won't help — we say no

- **Eyebrow:** When we say no
- **Headline:** We'd rather lose the work than sell you a pilot that can't win.
- **Body (as a short honest list):**
  - If a simple script would fix it, you don't need a pilot — and we'll tell you.
  - If the data isn't there yet, there's no clean number to move. We wait.
  - If no one on your side will own the result, the handover has nowhere to land.
- **Closing line:** Saying no up front is cheaper for both of us than a pilot that was never
  going to reach a number.
