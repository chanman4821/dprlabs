# Services & offerings — deep breakdown

*This is the source-of-truth for how DPR Labs describes what it offers. It feeds the Home
"What we do" section and the How it works page. It is **not** a services menu page — DPR Labs
sells **one offer, not a menu** (see `content/SITEMAP.md`).*

*Every offering below leads with the **customer outcome** — what you get, not what we do. Each
one names who it's for, what you get, **what success actually looks like**, how it runs, and
its honest limit. Grounded in the live site (`index.html` hero + "What we do", `method.html`,
`jarvis.html`) and the plan docs. No invented clients, results, or promised outcomes.*

---

## The shape of it — one offer, two ways we help after

DPR Labs is built for **one** thing and helps two more ways when it fits:

1. **The two-week pilot** — the low-risk way in. *(Entry offer.)*
2. **Agentic AI builds** — the core work the pilot proves and then scales. *(Primary.)*
3. **Investor intros** — a supporting help for founders, when it genuinely fits. *(Supporting.)*
4. **Product shipping** — idea to shipped product with the same team. *(Supporting.)*

The pilot and the builds are the spine. The pilot is how you test us cheaply; the build is the
real system it proves out and scales. Investor intros and product shipping are things we do for
founders we're **already** working with — not a second business, and never sold as a guarantee.

**The through-line:** every engagement is scoped small, agreed on a number where a number makes
sense, built on **your** systems from day one, and handed over so you own it. You can fire us at
any step and keep everything.

## 1 — The two-week pilot *(entry offer)*

- **Outcome:** In two weeks, a real number tells you whether AI can take one specific task off
  your team — at a fixed price agreed up front, with the code yours to keep whether it worked
  or not.
- **Who it's for:** A founder or operator with one repetitive, high-volume workflow — support
  replies, invoices, documents, data entry — that eats real hours every week, and a way to
  measure it.
- **What you get:**
  - One workflow, scoped to the biggest time saving, with the success number agreed **in
    writing before any build**.
  - A real, working agentic AI system built **inside your own infrastructure** — your cloud,
    your repo, your accounts — not a sandbox demo.
  - A scorecard: *before → after → sample size → method → caveat*, with the method published so
    you can re-check it yourself.
  - The code, weights, and docs handed to your team. No license, no seat, no lock-in.
- **What success looks like:** the one number you agreed on moved — measured on a real,
  human-checked sample with the method in plain sight — **or it didn't, and you don't pay the
  fee.** Either way you walk away owning the code. Success is defined before we start, not
  argued about after.
- **How it works / terms:** Fixed price band **$8,000–$15,000**, quoted up front. Timeline
  **~2–4 weeks** end to end (scoping, build, measurement, handover). Four steps: agree the
  number → build on your systems → measure honestly → hand it off. *(Full detail on the How it
  works page.)*
- **The guarantee:** if the pilot doesn't move the number we agreed, you don't pay the fee —
  and you keep the code. The guarantee is why an early-stage studio is safe to hire: the risk
  sits with us, not you.
- **When it's not a fit:** if a simple script would fix it, if the data isn't there to set a
  clean baseline, or if no one on your side will own the result — we'll tell you before you
  pay, even though it costs us the work.
- **CTA:** Book a 2-week pilot → `contact.html`

## 2 — Agentic AI builds *(primary)*

- **Outcome:** a working AI system doing a real job in your production — owned by your team and
  measured by the number you agreed — instead of a proof of concept that never ships.
- **Who it's for:** teams that have proven direction (often through the pilot) and want the real
  system built and hardened, on their own stack, without becoming dependent on a vendor.
- **What you get:**
  - Real agentic systems — the kind where specialised workers plan, build, grade, and repair
    the work — wired into your actual systems, not a chatbot bolted on the side.
  - The same discipline that runs the pilot: one number, a published method, a scorecard, and a
    senior human approving the calls that matter.
  - Everything built on **your** infra, repo, and accounts from day one, so there's nothing to
    migrate and nothing to rent at the end.
- **What success looks like:** the system is running the real job in your production, the number
  you agreed still holds when you re-check it, and your team can run and change the system
  **without us in the loop.** The proof isn't a demo — it's that you could fire us tomorrow and
  the thing keeps working.
- **How we build it:** on Jarvis, our multi-agent mesh — planner, dispatcher, builder, grader,
  healer. It's how a few senior people ship like a much larger team. We show it truthfully: a
  real, sanitized run from our own logs lives on the Built on Jarvis page — **305 files changed,
  60 tasks dispatched, ~18.4 hours of compute in one run** (source: `assets/data/jarvis-run.json`,
  no client data).
- **The honest limit:** a mesh speeds up disciplined, well-specified work. It does not replace
  judgment, and we don't claim autonomy we don't have. Some calls need a senior human, and we
  keep one there on purpose.
- **CTA:** See how we build → `jarvis.html`

## 3 — Investor intros *(supporting)*

- **Outcome:** warm, relevant introductions to investors and resources who actually fit your
  stage and sector — so your fundraising conversations start from a real connection, not a cold
  list.
- **Who it's for:** founders we're already working with, where an introduction is genuinely
  useful and we can make it honestly.
- **What you get:** when it fits, we connect you to the right people — warm, relevant, and
  targeted. No spray-and-pray, no mass forwarding, no selling your deck to a list.
- **What success looks like:** an introduction warm enough that the investor takes the call
  because they trust our read of the fit — not a forwarded deck sitting unread. What happens
  after the introduction is between you and the investor; a good intro is a real door opened,
  not a promised outcome.
- **How it works:** this is a best-effort help offered to founders in our orbit, not a
  fundraising service. We only make an introduction we'd stand behind on **both** sides — which
  means sometimes the honest answer is that we don't have the right person yet.
- **The honest limit — read this:** we do **not** promise a raise, a specific investor, an
  amount, or a timeline, and we do not claim a track record of funded rounds. An introduction is
  an introduction. If we can't help here, we'll say so plainly rather than pretend a network we
  don't have.
- **CTA:** Start a conversation → `contact.html`

## 4 — Product shipping *(supporting)*

- **Outcome:** you go from idea to a shipped product, built by the same senior team that runs
  the pilots — instead of hiring, managing, and hoping a separate agency ships.
- **Who it's for:** founders who need to actually build and launch a product, not just get
  advice or a slide deck.
- **What you get:** hands-on senior build work from idea to a real, shipped product, with the
  same honesty ethos as the pilot — clear scope, real systems, and you owning what we build.
- **What success looks like:** a real product in your users' hands, running on your stack, that
  your team owns and can extend — shipped by the people who built it, not tossed over a wall to
  a maintenance vendor. Where a number makes sense, the same one-number discipline tells you
  it's working.
- **How it works:** scoped with you up front, built on your stack, handed over so your team can
  run and extend it. The same one-number discipline applies wherever a number makes sense.
- **The honest limit:** we're a small, senior studio — we take the work we can do well and say
  no to the rest. If the scope needs a bigger team or a capability we don't have, we'll tell you
  rather than overcommit.
- **CTA:** Tell us what you're building → `contact.html`

## How the four connect (so it reads as one system, not a menu)

The pilot is the front door. It's deliberately cheap and bounded so a skeptical buyer can test
us on real work before committing. If the number moves and you want the real thing, that same
work becomes an **agentic AI build** — same team, same discipline, hardened for production and
handed to you to own. **Investor intros** and **product shipping** are how we keep helping the
founders we're already close to: an introduction when it genuinely fits, or a full product when
you need to ship, not just pilot. No engagement locks you into the next one — each ends with you
owning what we built.

---

## Why "outcome first" for every offering

Buyers don't buy agents, intros, or "shipping" — they buy fewer hours on invoices, a real
connection, a launched product. So every entry above starts with the result you get and mentions
the mechanism second. That's the same rule the landing-page plan sets: **lead with the outcome,
name the tech (and the limit) after.**
