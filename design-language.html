The one number

Inter · bold · tabular · amber — never mono, never decorative
42%

This is the treatment, not a published claim. The reserved amber is spent only on the single figure that carries a build's result — the real number comes from your agreed metric, measured before and after. Hierarchy is earned by size, weight and space; colour is not decoration.

House rules

strip the chrome and the hierarchy must still read

Do

  • One zone dominates each screen (60%+ of attention).
  • Hairline 1px detail; depth from surface value and light.
  • Spend the amber only on the one number and one active state.
  • Honest, anonymised-but-real proof only — cut what you can't back.

Never

  • Glow orbs, neon mesh, particle fields, rainbow gradients.
  • Symmetric equal-card bento or cockpit top+left+right rails.
  • Mono for prices, percentages, counters or headings.
  • Hierarchy from swapping font family.
