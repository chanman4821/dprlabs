The problem

AI that never ships, and capital that never connects

Most enterprise AI is slideware and pilots that die before production: billed by the hour, locked to a vendor, impossible to measure.

On the other side, strong founders can’t reach warm capital, and investors drown in un-vetted deal spam. Both sides are starved for the same thing: someone they can trust.

What we do

Two things, done properly

We build AI inside your own systems, agree on one number that says whether it worked, and hand it over. You pay a monthly retainer, not a one-off project fee.

  • Four kinds of work: automation, compliance & governance, low-code tools, and data integration.
  • We also introduce the founders we work with to investors we know. Introductions only. We never take a cut of a raise.

How we deliver

A small team that ships like a big one

We build with NR3, a separate AI platform we help design and test on real client work. It scopes the job, does it, checks the result, and fixes what's broken, so a few senior people can cover a lot of ground.

You still get a real person on the decisions that matter. And we don't hand you a demo. We hand you a working system your own team can run without us. (NR3 is its own company — see the NR3 one-pager.)

Why it's hard to copy

The two sides feed each other

Good consulting work makes a founder worth backing, which lets us introduce them to investors we trust.
When those bets go well, investors trust our picks more, so better founders and better clients come to us.

It runs on relationships, and those take years to earn. A consulting shop with no network has nothing to introduce. An intro service with no real work has no proof behind the names. Doing both well is the hard part.

Why now

Spending is up, trust is down

Companies are spending fast on AI but can't tell what's actually working. New rules are landing in exactly the regulated fields we focus on. And founders and investors both want a name they can trust instead of another cold list.

Team & posture

Small, senior, honest

You work with the people who build, not an account manager. We don't put fake clients, numbers, or logos on anything. On the capital side we're not a broker: no success fees, we disclose conflicts, and any startup we hold a stake in is a small, clearly-marked, unpaid part of what investors see.
