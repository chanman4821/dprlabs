The contract

Steal this sentence before you buy any AI pilot.

Every pilot starts with a metric contract. Not a strategy doc. Not “make us AI-first.” One sentence that can be read by finance, operations, and the person who will inherit the work. If the sentence sounds vague, the pilot is not ready.

Template: We will move ___ from ___ to ___ within two weeks, measured on ___, checked by ___.

Example: We will move support-ticket first-response resolution rate from 41% to 60% within two weeks, measured on a frozen holdout of 500 real tickets, checked by your ops lead running the eval suite we hand over.

The number is agreed in writing and does not change mid-pilot. It has to be something you already measure or can measure without theater. No moving goalposts. No basket of vanity stats. No blended “quality score” that lets everyone argue after the invoice.

Ownership

You own the system, not a login.

When the two weeks end, you own the whole thing. We do not hide the useful parts behind our account, our runtime, or our private prompts. If a vendor will not hand over the pieces, you are not buying an AI asset. You are renting a dependency with better branding.

Source code

Every line we wrote, in your repo, under your license, with no obfuscation and no mystery binary you cannot rebuild.

Weights and adapters

Any fine-tuned weights, LoRA adapters, and exact base-model references needed to re-serve the work without us.

Eval suite

The scoring harness and frozen test set that decides whether the number moved, so your team can rerun the verdict.

Prompts and config

All prompts, system messages, decoding settings, and parameter files, versioned with the reason each choice exists.

Deploy scripts

Containers or infrastructure scripts that stand the system up from zero in your accounts, not ours.

Data pipeline

The ingest, cleaning, and formatting code that turns raw operational data into what the model sees.

Runbook

A plain-English operating guide: how it works, how to refresh it, how to roll back, and what to inspect when the number drifts.

Honest limits

What two weeks cannot buy.

A two-week pilot can prove and hand over one owned improvement. It cannot make your company “AI-native.” That phrase mostly means nobody has agreed who owns the work after the demo.

  • It cannot fix more than one metric. A second number is a second pilot.
  • It cannot rebuild broken data. If the data to move the number does not exist, we decline the pilot.
  • It cannot deliver a fully hardened, on-call platform with a 99.99% service promise.
  • It cannot guarantee the gain holds forever. Models drift, workflows change, and the eval suite is how you catch it.
  • It cannot pass your formal compliance, security, or legal audit inside the same window.
  • It cannot move a number nobody can measure. No honest scoreboard, no pilot.

This is the controversial part: saying no is part of the product. We would rather reject a bad pilot in twenty minutes than sell you a beautiful failure.

Price

Fixed price. No move, no pay.

Most pilots land between $8,000 and $15,000, depending on how much data plumbing the one number needs. No hourly meter. No surprise line items. No scope-creep invoice after everyone is already emotionally committed.

If the agreed number does not reach the agreed target on the agreed check within two weeks, you owe nothing and keep every artifact produced along the way. The refund is real because the scoreboard is set before we take the work, and your data checks it. We only accept pilots where the number looks movable. We are betting our time against your outcome.

20-minute scoping call

The call decides if there is a real pilot.

You leave with a fixed price and start date, or an honest no. Both are useful. The agenda is intentionally narrow.

0-3 minutes

Name the one number and why it matters commercially.

3-8 minutes

8-13 minutes

Inspect the data source, access path, and whether it is clean enough for two weeks.

13-17 minutes

Define the frozen check that decides pass or fail.

17-20 minutes

Choose go or no-go, fixed price, start date, and owner.
