Why now

Companies want to own their AI, not rent a black box.

Spending on AI is up, but companies can’t tell what’s actually working. They are wary of vendors that lock them in. New rules are landing in exactly the regulated fields we focus on.

The problem

AI that never ships. Capital that never connects.

For companies

Most enterprise AI is slide decks and trials that die before production — billed by the hour, tied to one vendor, impossible to measure.

For founders & investors

Good founders can’t reach the right investors; investors drown in cold pitches. Both are starved for the same thing: someone they can trust.

What we do

Two things, done properly.

Consulting

  • We build AI inside your own systems.
  • We agree on one number that shows whether it worked.
  • We hand it over on a monthly retainer, not a one-off.
  • Four kinds of work: automation, compliance & governance, low-code tools, data integration.

Investor network

We introduce the founders we work with to investors we know — introductions only, never a cut of a raise.

The way in

Pick one number. We prove it moves. You own the code.

A two-week pilot, fixed $8k–$15k up front, one workflow and one success number agreed in writing, built inside your own systems. If the number doesn’t move, you don’t pay the fee — and you keep the code either way.

The risk sits with us.

Hard to copy

The two sides feed each other.

Good consulting work makes a founder worth backing. That lets us introduce them to investors we trust. When those bets pay off, investors trust our picks more, so better founders and clients come to us.

It runs on relationships, which take years to earn. A consulting shop with no network has nothing to introduce; an intro service with no real work has no proof.
