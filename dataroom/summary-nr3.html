The problem

AI coding tools are quick to a demo, then quietly ship the four things that break real software: slop (it compiles but does the wrong thing), made-up code (imports and paths that don't exist), stalling (it loops and burns money without finishing), and drift (a five-line request turns into a 500-line mess). Nothing checks the work, nothing remembers what happened, and there's no record of what changed.

What NR3 does

You describe what you want. NR3 breaks it into tasks, gives each one to the worker that's best at it, builds it, checks it, and fixes what fails, writing down every step. Each check is there to catch one of those four problems. Instead of reading through every code change, you get a short record of what it did and whether it passed. Full detail in the architecture doc.

How it works

One AI brain plans the work, then hands each task to the worker best suited to it, and anything that isn't good enough gets sent back, not shipped. It keeps a full record of every job and uses what worked before on the next one. Built-in stops keep it safe: a kill switch, a spending cap, a limit on how much runs at once, and tight control over the processes it starts.

Why it's hard to copy

Fast tools that skip the checking just create rework. NR3 does the opposite: it compares every result to what actually happened and remembers it, so it gets better with each job instead of drifting. Checking the work and learning from it is much harder to copy than a quick demo. And because NR3 runs on top of whichever AI models are best, it improves every time the models do, instead of competing with them.
