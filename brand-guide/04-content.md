# 4 · Content Guide (Value‑Based Selling)

How to sell **DPR AI** with content — lead with the buyer's problem and its cost, then prove DPR moves a real number and hands over something they own.

> **The story, in five beats:** Most teams are promised transformation and handed a demo that rots in production. The hidden cost isn't the tool — it's wasted internal time, a vendor you can't leave, and a "win" nobody can measure. Slop fails because it's built to look smart, not to move a number. DPR agrees on one metric first, wires the AI into your real systems, and hands you the code, weights, and tests — you own it. **If the number doesn't move, you don't pay.**

## Who we're talking to — and what hurts

- Bought or piloted AI that demos well but never reaches production or ROI.
- Locked into a vendor's API and pricing with no code, weights, or exit path.
- Can't tell leadership a single hard number the AI actually moved.
- Internal team is drowning; "add AI" became another half‑finished project.
- Founder with a real product idea but no senior team to ship an owned AI product.
- Drowning in AI hype and can't separate what works from what's slop.

## How we prove value on the page

- **Agree‑one‑number** contract shown up front — define the metric before any build.
- **Ownership handover** artifact — code, weights, tests, and docs transferred to the client.
- **Live teardown** of a real AI system showing where slop breaks in production.
- **Cost‑of‑slop calculator** the visitor runs on their own inputs.
- **No‑lock‑in guarantee** — runs on your infra, no proprietary runtime.
- **If‑it‑doesn't‑move‑you‑don't‑pay** risk reversal, stated on the page, not buried.

## Objections → reframes

| They say… | We say… |
|-----------|---------|
| AI agencies overpromise and I've been burned. | We agree on one number first and tie payment to it. If it doesn't move, you don't pay — the risk is ours. |
| We'll get locked into your platform. | You own the code, weights, and tests at handover. It runs on your infra — fire us tomorrow and it still works. |
| Custom AI is too expensive and slow. | We scope to one measured outcome, not a moonshot. You see cost against the number it moves. |
| Our data and systems are too messy. | Messy is normal — we build into real systems, not clean demos. The teardown shows how we handle the mess first. |
| We can just use ChatGPT / off‑the‑shelf. | Great for drafts, useless for a measured, owned outcome in your workflow. We build the part they can't. |
