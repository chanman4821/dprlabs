Why most AI projects stall

Most AI projects die as a demo.

Not because the tech can't work — because nobody agreed what "working" meant. A vendor sells a deck, runs a flashy proof of concept, and hands you something that never reaches production. Six months later you're still paying, still dependent, no closer to owning it. We built DPR Labs to be the opposite: small scope, one agreed number, fast, and yours to keep.

30-second self-check

Is your workflow actually pilot-ready?

Most "AI didn't work" stories are really "nobody agreed what working meant" stories. Answer four questions about your own workflow — you'll know in thirty seconds whether you have an AI problem or a scoping problem.

Answer honestly — there are no wrong answers, only useful ones.

Is there a number?Does this workflow produce something you can count — minutes, volume, error rate, cost?
Is it measured today?Could you tell me its current value this week, from real data?
Would moving it 20% matter?Would a fifth less time, or a fifth fewer errors, change a real decision or free real hours?
Can a human check the answer?Can someone on your team look at one output and tell whether the AI got it right?
