The state of the industry

The industry is drowning in AI slop.

Dead demos. Chatbots bolted on the side. Endpoints you rent forever. "Accuracy" with no baseline. Most "AI transformation" is theater billed as progress. Here are six kinds — and the tell that gives each one away.

The Bolt-On Bot

A GPT wrapper stapled to the corner of your page. Tell: it can't touch your real data or workflow.

The Dead Demo

Flawless on the sales call, never seen in production. Tell: no live metric, no user count, no incident history.

The Rented Brain

You pay per call forever and own nothing. Tell: no weights, no code, no exit path in the contract.

The Vanity Accuracy

"98% accurate" with no baseline. Tell: no sample size, no comparison group, no confidence interval.

AI-First Theater

A rebrand with the word "AI" and zero shipped behavior change. Tell: the roadmap is slides, not commits.

The Eval-Free Ship

A model in production with no test suite. Tell: nobody can say if last week's change made it better or worse.

Why most AI projects stall

Most AI projects die as a demo.

Not because the tech can't work — because nobody agreed what "working" meant. A vendor sells a deck, runs a flashy proof of concept, and hands you something that never reaches production. Six months later you're still paying, still dependent, no closer to owning it. We built DPR Labs to be the opposite: small scope, one agreed number, fast, and yours to keep.

30-second self-check

Is your workflow actually pilot-ready?

Most "AI didn't work" stories are really "nobody agreed what working meant" stories. Answer four questions about your own workflow — you'll know in thirty seconds whether you have an AI problem or a scoping problem.

Answer honestly — there are no wrong answers, only useful ones.
